Lily ray 4mo edited report this post so much of what i see as the right way to approach ai search optimization (aeo, geo etc.) is to ensure that the most important details about your brand, products, services, etc. Are included and presented in your owned assets (website, social media, other online profiles). This is not rocket science, nor is it a massive departure from the work seo teams have been doing for a long time - especially teams focused on promoting e-e-a-t. But i do think there are certain ways to present additional, deeper information that could potentially be asked about in llms (or retrieved via fan-out queries), above and beyond what people may have typed into search engines. For ai search optimization, as a brand, you want to get ahead of answering all questions people might be asking about you by controlling the conversation on your own website. As an example, i use my personal site often for tests with seo & ai search. I recently launched a page to list all my podcast and webinar appearances (although it’s nowhere near complete 😅) today i saw in google’s newest ai search web feature - web guide - that google included a dedicated breakout section specifically for my podcast and webinar appearances. I doubt it would have created that section if i didn’t organize all the content into one place. I think there are way more opportunities than a simple “about us” page to expand on all the things people should know about your brand.
