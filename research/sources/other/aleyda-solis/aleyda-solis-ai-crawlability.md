# Aleyda Solís: AI Search Crawlability and Multi-Platform Visibility (2026)

*Source: Humans of Martech Podcast (Jan 13, 2026)*
*Link: [humansofmartech.com/2026/01/13/202-aleyda-solis-ai-search-crawlability/](https://humansofmartech.com/2026/01/13/202-aleyda-solis-ai-search-crawlability/)*

## Key Research Findings

### 1. The "Silent Block" and AI Crawlability
*   **Raw HTML vs. JS:** AI crawlers (OpenAI, Perplexity, etc.) primarily ingest raw HTML and often do not render JavaScript. Content injected via JS is invisible to many LLMs.
*   **CDN Blocks:** Many websites unknowingly block AI crawlers at the CDN or hosting level (e.g., Cloudflare default settings).
*   **Action:** Ensure core content is accessible in the initial HTML response.

### 2. SEO as a Multi-Platform Discovery System
*   **Platform Fragmentation:** Search is shifting from Google-only to a mix of TikTok, Instagram, and LLMs.
*   **Ambient Signals:** LLMs prioritize "ambient signals of trust"—mentions on Reddit, Discord, and YouTube comments—over traditional backlinks.

### 3. Content Strategy for the AI Era
*   **The TOFU Crisis:** Generic "Top of Funnel" (TOFU) content is easily summarized by AI, leading to zero-click searches.
*   **"Texture" and Information Gain:** To survive, content must include original research, case studies, and brand-specific expertise that AI cannot replicate.
*   **Topic Clustering:** Focus on owning entire topics and understanding brand sentiment within those clusters rather than chasing individual keyword volume.

### 4. Measurement and Reporting
*   **Lack of Data:** LLMs currently lack "Search Consoles" because they have little incentive to show referral traffic until they monetize through ads.
*   **Proxy Metrics:** Brands should quantify LLM traffic manually and test how they appear across different user personas.

## Summary of 2026 Playbook
Aleyda argues that SEOs must move beyond keyword volume and focus on **Technical Accessibility for AI Bots** and **Community-Led Brand Authority**. The goal is to be "Top of Algorithmic Mind" by being mentioned in the places where LLMs "listen."
