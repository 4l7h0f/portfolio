## 1. Overview
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the framework for building a high-authority, AI-optimized content engine for B2B SaaS.

## 2. Workflow (Step-by-step SOP)
### Step 1 — Keyword & Intent Research
- Identify target keywords using SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, GSC)
- Prioritize search intent over volume
- Group keywords into clusters

> “Focus on search intent over keyword volume” (source: Brian Dean, https://backlinko.com/search-intent, 12.03.2023)

---

### Step 2 — SERP & AI Landscape Analysis
- Analyze top-ranking pages (Google SERP)
- Identify:
  - Content structure (H1–H3)
  - Content angle
- Check how AI answers (ChatGPT / Google AI) respond to the query

> “Search is shifting from links to AI-generated answers” (source: :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}, 2025)

---

### Step 3 — AI-Assisted Content Creation
- Use AI to:
  - Generate outlines
  - Draft content
- Do NOT publish raw AI output

> “AI should assist drafting, not replace human editing” (source: Google Search Central, 2023)

---

### Step 4 — Human Editing & Expertise Layer
- Add:
  - Real experience
  - Examples
  - Opinions
- Ensure E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust)

---

### Step 5 — Structure for AI Retrieval
- Write content in:
  - Clear sections
  - Direct answers
- Optimize for:
  - Summarization
  - Semantic clarity
- Use structured data where possible

> “AI models rely on external retrieval systems” (source: :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} citing :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}, 2025)

---

### Step 6 — On-Page SEO Optimization
- Optimize:
  - Title tags
  - Meta descriptions
  - Headings
  - Internal linking

---

### Step 7 — Brand Authority Building
- Increase:
  - Brand mentions
  - Content consistency
  - Presence across platforms
- Focus on trust and recognition

> “AI search changes how users discover and trust brands” (source: :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}, 2025)

---

### Step 8 — Publish & Index
- Publish content
- Submit to Google Search Console
- Ensure indexing and crawlability

---

### Step 9 — Continuous Optimization Loop (Weekly)
- Run automated checks (GSC + AI tools)
- Identify:
  - Low CTR pages
  - Content decay
  - Cannibalization
- Apply quick wins (e.g., title updates)

> “Automation enables continuous SEO optimization instead of quarterly audits” (source: :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}, 2026)

---

### Step 10 — AI Visibility Tracking
- Monitor:
  - Mentions in AI answers
  - Brand presence in LLM outputs
- Track beyond rankings:
  - Visibility
  - Influence
  - Conversions

> “Ranking is no longer the only KPI—AI visibility matters” (source: :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}, 2025)

## 3. Tools & Stack

- Ahrefs
- Google Search Console
- Google Trends

- Ahrefs → keyword difficulty, competitor gaps

Goal: Understand what ranks AND what AI surfaces

#### Tools:

- ChatGPT
- Perplexity AI

- SEO now = being retrievable by AI, not just ranking

#### Tools:

- Notion

## 4. Detailed Execution Guidelines (with sources)
This section translates the workflow and SOP rules into **actionable execution steps** with clear instructions and referenced insights from the provided sources.

---

### 4.1 Research (Finding Opportunities)

**Steps:**

1. Extract keyword opportunities from Google Search Console (GSC)

   * Focus on:

     * High impressions
     * Low CTR
     * Positions between 3–10

2. Identify content gaps using Ahrefs or similar tools

   * Look for:

     * Competitor keywords
     * Low difficulty + high intent queries

3. Validate trends using Google Trends

   * Prioritize:

     * Rising topics
     * Consistent demand

**Execution Rule:**

* Prioritize **real data (GSC)** over theoretical keyword tools

**Source Insight:**

* Gael Breton → High impressions + low CTR pages = quick wins

---

### 4.2 SERP & AI Analysis

**Steps:**

1. Search target keyword in Google

   * Analyze:

     * Top 5 results
     * Content format (list, guide, comparison)
     * Title patterns

2. Query the same topic in AI tools (ChatGPT / Perplexity)

   * Observe:

     * Which brands/content are cited
     * Structure of answers

3. Identify:

   * Content gaps
   * Missing angles
   * Weak competitors

**Execution Rule:**

* Always analyze **both traditional SERP and AI outputs**

**Source Insight:**

* Jason Barnard → SEO now includes **AI visibility and trust signals**

---

### 4.3 Content Creation (AI-Assisted)

**Steps:**

1. Generate outline using AI

2. Expand into full draft

3. Manually refine:

   * Add examples
   * Add opinions
   * Improve clarity

4. Ensure:

   * Clear structure (H1–H3)
   * Direct answers to queries
   * Scannable format

**Execution Rule:**

* NEVER publish raw AI output
* ALWAYS add human context and expertise

**Source Insight:**

* Discussion consensus → AI reduces friction but **does not replace judgment**

---

### 4.4 Optimization (SEO + AI)

**Steps:**

1. Optimize title tag

   * Include:

     * Primary keyword
     * Curiosity or benefit

2. Improve meta description

   * Focus on:

     * Click appeal (CTR)

3. Fix CTR leaks

   * Identify via GSC
   * Update title/snippet

4. Improve internal linking

   * Link to:

     * Relevant pages
     * Supporting content

5. Refresh outdated content

   * Update:

     * Data
     * Examples
     * Structure

**Execution Rule:**

* Prioritize **CTR optimization first** before creating new content

**Source Insight:**

* Gael Breton → Title tag update alone can unlock major gains
* LinkedIn comments → Optimization becomes **continuous, not quarterly**

---

### 4.5 Distribution & Authority Building

**Steps:**

1. Share content on:

   * LinkedIn
   * Twitter (X)
   * Reddit

2. Engage in discussions

   * Provide value
   * Reference your content when relevant

3. Build authority signals:

   * Mentions
   * Backlinks
   * Brand presence

**Execution Rule:**

* Focus on **visibility beyond your website**

**Source Insight:**

* Jason Barnard → AI pulls from broader web signals, not just websites

---

### 4.6 Monitoring & Weekly Iteration

**Steps:**

1. Run weekly audit:

   * Check GSC for:

     * CTR drops
     * New opportunities

2. Identify:

   * Content decay
   * Cannibalization
   * Dead pages

3. Apply quick fixes:

   * Title updates
   * Content refresh
   * Internal linking

4. Track:

   * CTR improvement
   * Ranking changes

**Execution Rule:**

* Run optimization **weekly (not quarterly)**

**Source Insight:**

* Gael Breton → Automation enables **continuous SEO operations**

## 5. Where experts disagree
This section highlights **key debates in modern SEO (especially AI-driven SEO)** based on the provided sources and industry perspectives. These disagreements are important because they define **strategic decisions**, not just execution.

---

### 5.1 AI Replaces SEO Consultants vs AI Augments Them

**Position A — Replacement (Automation-first):**

* AI tools can:

  * Detect CTR issues
  * Identify content decay
  * Find cannibalization
* Tasks that used to cost $100–200/hour can now be automated

👉 Argument:

* SEO becomes **repeatable and scalable**
* High-frequency optimization (weekly/daily) becomes possible

**Position B — Augmentation (Human-first):**

* AI lacks:

  * Strategic thinking
  * Business context
  * Prioritization judgment

👉 Argument:

* AI accelerates execution, but **does not replace expertise**

**Conclusion:**
AI replaces **execution-heavy tasks**, not **strategic roles**

---

### 5.2 Automation vs Manual SEO

**Position A — Automation Wins:**

* Reduces friction
* Enables continuous optimization
* Surfaces quick wins instantly

👉 Example:

* Weekly automated audits vs quarterly manual audits

---

**Position B — Manual Still Matters:**

* Humans develop:

  * Pattern recognition
  * Deeper SERP intuition
* Over-automation risks:

  * Missing nuance
  * Blind optimization

---

**Conclusion:**
Best approach = **Hybrid system**

* Automation → detection
* Human → decision

---

### 5.3 Frequency of SEO Work

**Position A — Continuous SEO (Modern View):**

* SEO should run:

  * Weekly
  * Even daily

👉 Argument:

* Cost of checking is near zero

---

**Position B — Periodic SEO (Traditional View):**

* Quarterly audits are sufficient

👉 Argument:

* SEO changes slowly
* Avoid unnecessary changes

---

**Conclusion:**
Modern SEO shifts toward **continuous optimization**

### 5.4 What Matters More: Ranking vs Visibility in AI

**Conclusion:**
SEO is evolving from:

### 5.5 Content Quantity vs Content Quality

* AI enables:

**Position B — Depth & Authority:**

### 5.6 Data-Driven SEO vs Intuition-Based SEO

  * GSC
  * CTR
  * Rankings

* Data → direction
